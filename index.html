Our History

Cafe Street, Kerala (1930s - Present)
In the 1930s, Cafe Street began as a small cluster of tea stalls in a quiet corner of Kerala, catering to local workers and traders. Over the decades, it evolved into a vibrant social hub. During the 1950s and 60s, the street became known for its traditional Kerala snacks like banana fritters and masala chai, drawing in more visitors. The 1970s saw the introduction of Western influences, with cafes offering coffee and pastries, attracting a younger crowd.
By the 1990s, Cafe Street had transformed into a blend of old and new, where traditional Kerala cuisine coexisted with international flavors. The street's popularity soared, becoming a must-visit spot for tourists. Today, Cafe Street stands as a cultural landmark, renowned for its rich history, diverse food offerings, and a lively atmosphere that captures the essence of Kerala's evolving culinary scene.
